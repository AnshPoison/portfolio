ASSET

I possess extensive experience in the realm of game asset creationI am also well-versed in designing modular models tailored to game design needs. My commitment to excellence in delivering professional-grade assets enables me to meet and exceed the demands of the dynamic gaming industry, ensuring that each project is crafted with meticulous attention to detail and utmost creativity.

My primary focus lies in utilizing industry-leading applications such as Blender and Substance Painter, while occasionally incorporating 3D Coat into my workflow as well.

Models

(High Poly & Low Poly)

I specialize in the comprehensive development of game-ready 3D models, orchestrating the entire process from initial high-poly concepts to meticulously crafted low-poly renditions. With a keen focus on precision, I seamlessly transition these models into their final, optimized forms, ensuring they are finely tuned for interactive experiences. My expertise extends to the intricate art of texturing, where I imbue each model with life and vibrancy, enhancing their visual allure while preserving their performance integrity. By overseeing the entire creative journey, from conceptual inception to the polished end product, I deliver a holistic and tailored approach to 3D asset development. My portfolio showcases a diverse array of projects that underscore my proficiency in this realm.

Sketchfab

I am engaged in the development of community-oriented models, with a primary focus on crafting an extensive array of game-ready assets. These meticulously designed assets are made freely accessible to the community through the Sketchfab platform. Additionally, I augment these models by providing accompanying textures, thereby enriching the overall quality and utility of the shared resources.

ANSH SINGLA

I am Ansh Singla, a passionate 3D modeler and game artist. Welcome to my creative corner! I'm thrilled to share my journey as a 3D artist with you. From a young age, I've been captivated by the magic of turning imagination into tangible art.
